Chapter 1

Water Pump for Umutnana

East Nusa Tenggara, Indonesia

Previous Next

More than 700 people in Umutnana Village, East Nusa Tenggara, lack access to clean water. They have to walk two kilometers from their houses for access to the water source. Each day they need to make at least three trips which is equals to at least four hours of their precious time, the time they could have spent to do something more productive rather than to get life’s basic need.

“It was never enough water for their homes, and it left them little time to earn money, study, and achieve their dreams.”

Our Solution

Solar Chapter will collaborate with PAMSIMAS (A National Platform for Rural Water Supply and Sanitation) to provide easier access of clean water. We plan to build a reservoir to collect water from three water sources, which will then deliver the water to the distribution tank near the village by using solar pumps. From distribution tank, the water will be distributed to 16 locations across the village.



Thanks to you, we made this dream come true.

Our Result

On the 9th-14th January 2018, Solar Chapter implemented its first chapter. With a plan and design at hand, we journeyed to Umutnana Village. We were received positively by the villagers, as we came to fulfill a promise we made in August 2017, that we would ease their efforts in obtaining clean water. As we built our solar water pump, the villagers were eager to help, aiding us in digging a route for the pipes, and constructing the solar panels. Now the villagers of Umutnana don’t have to walk 2 km daily to obtain water. Instead, the water will be available at 16 different public faucets, available in less than 10 m from their homes.

"This is not the end of chapter one, this is just the beginning part of the chapter."
